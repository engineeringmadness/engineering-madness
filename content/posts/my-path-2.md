## Tryst With Destiny

So how did I find the answer to life's most profound question – why?

The answer lies in the movie F1 which coincidentally released end of July. I am not going to go into spoilers (watch the movie yourself trust me bro!) but the movie revolves around return of a failed F1 driver now in his 60s. The main protagonist Sonny Hayes was poised to be the next big thing after Schumacher and Senna but a severe crash ends his career abruptly. Ever since then he's been living like a nomad, gambling and freelancing as a driver for various racing series. He is headed down this path until he gets a chance to return to F1 for a 9 race stint when his best friend from F1 needs help saving a struggling team. Again and again, throughout the film many of the characters ask the same question, Who is Sonny Hayes really and why is he back ?

The answer - He is chasing a feeling, when everything goes quite and he is flying on the track. A feeling of conquering one's doubts. I believe what is he describing is a state of flow that he feels when he is pushing beyond his limits in a new racing format. He still wants to prove that He can still be the best but its not to others its to himself. He is not interested in sponsors or media coverage just the racing. In the opening sequence, Sonny Hayes races the 24 hours of Daytona but during the night shift, which means literally the audience has gone home.

## This is Why we do it

State of flow for me comes when I am crafting a feature, I am molding and reshaping the structure of the code while running tests to ensure everything works. Its when I am designing the abstractions to ensure this functionality can survive in the long term and can be extended without the need for rewrites in the future. Whether I was working on Backend, Frontend or Data pipelines, that state of flow is what I have always been chasing. Just like Sonny Hayes leaves after winning F1 to pursue a new adventure, I too am not one to choose a specialization and settle into it. I am also chasing new challenges which has me shifting (albeit at a slower pace) from one project to another while a lot of my colleagues are still doing the very same thing. 

I realized that this rat race I thought I was destined to be part of chasing a higher package, better title, more visibility was a lie. I have never enjoyed giving fluffy presentations, I always get akward when I receive praise, I am in my element when no-one is watching and I am building software. 

## So where do I go from here ? 

Chasing a specialization (as that will get rid of the challenge of a new domain) promotion (As an individual contributor I maybe have 1-2 more promotion left during my entire career) or job security (becoming a mindless drone just to have a regular pay check)all are a no go. I will look for the next challenge where I can return to the state of flow I was missing dearly last year. I want to ensure that I maintain my health and fitness and gather up savings / investments to ensure I can continue on this path without being bothered by liabilities.
