## Tryst With Destiny

So how did I find the answer to life's most profound question – why?

The answer lies in the movie F1, which coincidentally released at the end of July. I am not going to go into spoilers (watch the movie yourself, trust me bro!), but the movie revolves around the return of a failed F1 driver now in his 60s. The main protagonist, Sonny Hayes, was poised to be the next big thing after Schumacher and Senna, but a severe crash ends his career abruptly. Ever since then, he's been living like a nomad, gambling and freelancing as a driver for various racing series. He is headed down this path until he gets a chance to return to F1 for a 9-race stint when his best friend from F1 needs help saving a struggling team. Again and again, throughout the film, many of the characters ask the same question: Who is Sonny Hayes really, and why is he back?

The answer: he is chasing a feeling, when everything goes quiet and he is flying on the track—a feeling of conquering one's doubts. I believe what he is describing is a state of flow / being in the zone that he feels when he is pushing beyond his limits in a new racing format. He still wants to prove that he can be the best, but it's not to others—it's to himself. He is not interested in sponsors or media coverage, just the racing. In the opening sequence, Sonny Hayes races the 24 Hours of Daytona during the night shift, which means literally the audience has gone home.

## This is Why we do it

The state of flow for me comes when I am crafting a feature, molding and reshaping the structure of the code while running tests to ensure everything works. It's when I am designing abstractions to ensure this functionality can survive in the long term and can be extended without the need for rewrites in the future. Whether I was working on backend, frontend, or data pipelines, that state of flow is what I have always been chasing. Just like Sonny Hayes leaves after winning F1 to pursue a new adventure, I too am not one to choose a specialization and settle into it. I am also chasing new challenges, which has me shifting (albeit at a slower pace) from one project to another, while a lot of my colleagues are still doing the very same thing.

I realized that this rat race I thought I was destined to be part of—chasing a higher package, better title, more visibility—was a lie. I have never enjoyed giving fluffy presentations, I always get awkward when I receive praise, and I am in my element when no one is watching and I am building software.

## So where do I go from here ? 

Chasing a specialization (as that will get rid of the challenge of a new domain), promotion (as an individual contributor I maybe have 1-2 more promotions left during my entire career), or job security (becoming a mindless drone just to have a regular paycheck)—all are a no-go. I will look for the next challenge where I can return to the state of flow I was missing dearly last year. I want to ensure that I maintain my health and fitness and gather up savings/investments to ensure I can continue on this path without being bothered by liabilities.

I don't know yet where this path will lead, but I feel good about it in my gut. It's that eureka moment when what I have known for a while finally clicks. People like DHH, Uncle Bob, and Kent Beck have been talking about the same things for many years, and it finally makes sense to me as well.
