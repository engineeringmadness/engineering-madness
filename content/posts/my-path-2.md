## Tryst With Destiny

Why this re-occuring question of a specialization within Software Engineering ? Its kind of what everyone just does. Its how you advance your career by going deep on one topic. But what am I after really ? Is it salary ? or a promotion ? or a hefty title ?

The answer lies in the movie F1, which coincidentally released. I am not going to go into spoilers (watch the movie yourself, trust me!), but the movie revolves around the return of a failed F1 driver now in his 60s. The main protagonist, Sonny Hayes, who was poised to be the next big thing after Schumacher and Senna, but a severe crash ends his career abruptly. Ever since then, he's been living like a nomad, gambling and freelance racing. He is headed down this path until he gets a chance to return to F1.

Sonny in one of the scenes when asked what's his story says he just loves driving. It doesn't matter what car he is driving, what circuit he is on. He doesn't need anyone to watch. He is just chasing a feeling, when everything goes quiet, everything slows down and he is flying on the track. The feeling of conquering one's doubts. I believe what he is describing is a state of flow / being in the zone.

## This is what I do

I get that exact feeling when I am writing code, not all the time but sometimes when I really care about what I am building, I know what is to be done now and can start to visualize what could change in the future. A sort of clarity emerges. Whether I am working on backend, frontend, or data pipelines, that state of flow has comes for me in unexpected and unplanned ways. 

Just like Sonny Hayes who leaves after winning his first race in F1 to pursue a new adventure, I too am not one to choose a specialization and settle into it. I am also chasing new challenges constantly, which has me shifting (albeit at a slower pace) from one project to another

## When Things click

I realize now that this rat race I thought I was destined to be part of—chasing a higher package, better title, more visibility—was a lie. I have never enjoyed any of that and I don't work as hard as I do because of any of that stuff, I do it because I simply love the feeling I get when I am plugged in writing code.

I want to find the next challenge where I can return to the state of flow I was missing dearly last year. I want to ensure that I maintain my health and fitness and gather up savings/investments to ensure I can continue on this path without being bothered by liabilities.

I don't know yet where this path will lead, but I feel good about it in my gut. It's that eureka moment when what I have known for a while finally clicks. People like DHH, Uncle Bob, and Kent Beck have been talking about the same things for many years, and I have been listening but it has finally clicked in my head now and I am absolutely pumped for what comes tomorrow.
